WeAreUganda: City to unveil Aguero statue on 10th anniversary of title-winning goal

Sports We Are Uganda 07 April 2022

Sergio Aguero

Manchester City will pay tribute to their all-time leading goalscorer Sergio Aguero by unveiling a statue of him outside the Etihad Stadium next month on the 10th anniversary of his goal that ended the club's 44-year wait for a league title. City reversed a 2-1 deficit to beat Queens Park Rangers 3-2 on the final day of the 2011-12 season, scoring two goals in stoppage time, with Aguero netting the winner to clinch City's first Premier League title.

Aguero scored a club record 260 goals in 390 appearances during a decade-long association with City, helping them win four more Premier League trophies, one FA Cup and six League Cups. He left City at the end of last season and joined Barcelona on a free transfer but the 33-year-old was forced to retire from soccer in December due to a heart condition.

Aguero's statue will be unveiled on 13 May as part of City's celebrations to mark the 10th anniversary of their first Premier League title. City unveiled statues of Aguero's former teammates David Silva and Vincent Kompany in August.
